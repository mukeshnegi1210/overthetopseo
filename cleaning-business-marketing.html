Cleaning Business Marketing

Your cleaning business needs a marketing partner who understands what works in your market. By selecting an expert team and using marketing strategies proven to work in cleaning business marketing, your comprehensive marketing plan will yield quality results. While the above are significant first steps and essential components to any cleaning company’s marketing plan, there are other strategies you may want to consider, including things you can do as the business owner or manager to bring new clients to your company.

Networking

Building a network of those who can help attract new clients to your cleaning business is an important marketing strategy. First, start by joining some local associations. Usually, your small-business bureau will have information about local groups that meet regularly. Look into trade associations in your area. By joining forces with other service-oriented business, you can trade referrals. For example, your clients may be looking for someone to help take care of their lawn or pool or to help with remodeling or renovation projects. Those professionals’ clients may also be in the market for a house cleaner.

When you recommend them to your clients, they are likely to reciprocate. Becoming active in trade groups is also a great way to learn more about running a small business, as you can learn from others about marketing, bookkeeping, inventory management, and other managerial tasks. Connect with local realtors, who often recommend house cleaning services to new homeowners. Homebuilders often hire cleaning services to prepare houses once construction is complete. If you have the workforce and capacity, consider connecting with a commercial real estate agent, so they can connect you with clients who need offices cleaned.

Referrals and Reviews

One excellent source of referrals is your current clients who are satisfied with the work you do for them. Asking your current clients to refer you to their friends and family is a proven marketing strategy. When you clean a home, leave a referral card your current client can give out, including a note explaining your request. Offer your current clients a referral incentive for sending new business your way.

This could be a discount on their current service for a specified time or a free bonus service like window washing or a carpet cleaning. Clients will appreciate you are willing to reward them, and you are guaranteed to continue bookings with them for them to redeem their bonus. If you have a client who is also in a service business, consider offering to trade referrals.

Before hiring a cleaning service to come into their home or business, most people will read online reviews. It is essential that you are aware of all of the reviews online about your cleaning business and that you actively cultivate good reviews.

The first step is claiming your business on Google, Yelp, Angie’s List, and other review sites. Make sure all information about your cleaning company is accurate. Next, respond to all reviews, and if you occasionally have a negative review, be sure to reach out to correct the problem. Don’t try to engage the negative reviewer through the online platform but instead do it privately. Do whatever is necessary to remedy the station, then ask them to please review you again. When you have happy clients, suggest they consider your cleaning services online. You can remind them to evaluate your services in person, via email, or through a postcard or other mailer. You can even provide them with detailed instructions for how to review you on various platforms to be sure they know how. Because many people trust online reviews, taking the time to understand how you are being examined to cultivate positive reviews is very important for successful marketing.

Do Great Work and Sell Your Unique Message

In the cleaning market, there is no greater marketing strategy than you and the quality of your work. When you do good work, hire good employees, train them well, and stand by your services, your clients will notice and recommend you to others. They will be more likely to review you positively online as well as hire you for additional services. Keeping an existing client is a free marketing strategy, so don’t overlook it. Also, ask your clients honestly what problem they have that you can help solve and what services you could offer that would be of interest to them. They are a great source for information about how to improve your business.

When marketing your house cleaner company, remember to focus on what makes you unique among all the rest. Use your social media posts, blog, and other online outlets to explain your unique place in the cleaning market, differentiating you from your competitors. Do you use environmentally friendly products? Do you offer deep-clean services? Are you able to clean especially tricky messes? Do you have more experience than others? Do you provide flexible scheduling? Do you also offer services such as grocery delivery or meal preparation? Show potential clients why yours is the right service for them and increase the value of your business above others.

Walk-Arounds and Fliers

These two simple strategies are easy ways to market your cleaning company’s services, and once you have developed the flier or door hanger and paid for printing, it is another low-cost, high-yield way to attract new clients quickly. It’s known by many names, but we’ll refer to it as the walk-around. If you or your employees are finished cleaning one of your client’s homes, take a few extra minutes to walk around the area and hang fliers or door hangers on neighboring homes.

You can focus on a specific number of houses (5, 9, 13, etc.) or a particular area (all the houses on this block), whatever works for you. This is a great way to focus on homes in the income brackets you are catering toward, and an easy to way make your schedule convenient, as you’ll spend less time traveling if your clients are concentrated in one area. Also, if potential clients see your car (with signs for your business on the side) in their neighborhood, they will know you are a trusted service provider among their neighbors. Likewise, hanging fliers in key areas in your community can yield good results.

Be sure your flier contains all the relevant information for your cleaning business, that it’s attractive but succinct, and that it tells how you can solve a problem they currently have. After developing and printing the fliers, put them up around your community in places where potential clients gather. Bulletin boards in office buildings and grocery stores are good as well as local gyms. Community events, festivals, and fairs are great opportunities to get out in front of the community, putting a face to your cleaning business’ name. Ask your networking friends to pass out fliers to their clients, or hang on in their offices.>

Email

Email campaigns can be beneficial for retaining current clients as well as bringing in new ones. Email allows you to build a more intimate relationship with current and potential clients by communicating with them directly and helping them solve problems they may have. Include cleaning tips or informational guides in each newsletter or on your blog, highlighting your expertise and also providing clients with helpful information.

Email links to useful videos or pictures and bios of you and your team, which helps customers know who they are working with. Email campaigns are a great way to introduce services you offer or ask for feedback on your performance or offerings. Targeted email campaigns can be directed to people who have visited your website or social media page but have yet to book a service. This is called remarketing, and it’s a very useful marketing strategy for booking new clients. You want to remind them what problem you can help solve for them, why they visited your website in the first place, and offer an incentive for booking with you the first time. This type of follow up is crucial for setting you apart from your competition.

There are many different companies and software that can help you set up targeted marketing emails, including addressing potential clients at each stage of the buying process. However, working with a cleaning company marketer with experience in email marketing is a good idea, as it will help yield the best results.

How to Advertise Your Cleaning Business

Google AdWords
Investing in a Pay-Per-Click (PPC) campaign on Google while you wait for your website to start generating organic search results is a good strategy to consider. You can narrow your focus to your geographic area, you can customize your keywords to highlight specific services you offer, and you can generate new leads for your growing business with this technique. AdWords can be more expensive, so you will have to weigh the pros and cons of this strategy against your business goals and marketing budget.

Direct Mail
Direct mail is also a little more expensive than other strategies to market your cleaning business, but it can also be a great way to find new clients. By purchasing lists from reputable places that include new homeowners, working parents, and people in specific income brackets, you are likely to be addressing your target market. Direct mailers, once designed, can be used for a long time for many different purposes.

Use Your Vehicle

Including a sign on your car to market your cleaning company is a great way to reach over 40,000 potential customers each day. Car wraps are long-lasting, magnet clings are inexpensive and easy to change, and other options, including window decals, are good choices. Keep it simple, with just your logo, phone number, and website, as too much information isn’t needed.
