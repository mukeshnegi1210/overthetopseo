Make a good first and second impression

Your website, office team, and first visit will define your new patient’s experience. To help newbies feel welcome, make sure your site is user-friendly and inviting. Consider adding a video tour of your office, or video introductions for your team members. Many of your first-time website visitors will be accessing your site on a mobile device, so definitely make sure your website is designed to be mobile responsive, guaranteeing a positive experience for users across all platforms.

The goal is to make sure the person visiting your site has a seamless and frictionless experience and is so happy with what they just saw that they want to become patients of yours! A new patient’s first visit is just as important as the digital experience. Make sure your office team is trained to be hospitable for all incoming patients and find small ways to help those first-timers feel welcome. One option is to offer New Patient Dinners, where you periodically host your newest clients to join you for a group meal. This is a fantastic opportunity to discuss holistic wellness, dig into the benefits of chiropractic care, and establish a relational connection with your customers.

Reward your clients

Everybody loves to feel appreciated! Loyalty programs allow you to demonstrate how much you value your patients, providing valuable incentives for return visits. Consider offering a free session after a certain number of visits, or perhaps a massage treatment. The same concept can be applied to new patients — referral programs allow you to simultaneously reward your existing clients, while also earning new business.

For example, you could give the new client and referring client one free adjustment each for their participation in the referral program. Regardless of the method, you choose, providing some incentive for your new and existing clients is a great way to remind them that they are valued and to keep them coming back for your services.

Stay in touch

There are a variety of different outlets you can use to keep your active and inactive patients engaged. A combination of newsletters, email campaigns, and social media updates is a great way to engage your client base and consistently remind them that you’re doing great work, regardless of whether or not they’re coming in for regular adjustments.

Not sure what information to share? The sky’s the limit! Use these updates to provide details for upcoming events, share wellness tips, celebrate patient stories, share one of your favorite healthy recipes, link to a new study about the benefits of chiropractic care, or simply invite them back in for another visit. The idea is to give more than you ask for. At the very least, these communications will remind your clients that you are there for them, and the consistent messaging will put your practice at the forefront of their minds for potential referrals or future needs!

Get involved

As a chiropractor, you have a lot of expertise to offer your local community! In addition to joining your local Chamber of Commerce and networking with other professionals, identify a few creative opportunities to educate the members of your community! Perhaps you could visit a local school, provide brief massage treatments for the teachers, and lead a seminar about the benefits of chiropractic adjustments for teachers.

Your practice could rent a booth at a community festival, hand out some freebies, and offer some free minor adjustments. You know your field and work better than anyone, so try and come up with your own unique ways to get involved! However you participate, you will be providing a meaningful service to your community and educating your neighbors, while also increasing your practice’s visibility in the local area.

Share your knowledge

Chiropractic care is a niche area of wellness, so put your expertise to good use. There are many ways to share your experience, including ebooks, traditional books, and instructional videos. By sharing your expertise, you’re able to demonstrate the depth of your knowledge, increase the credibility of your practice, and identify new patients that might benefit from your services!

Another huge benefit of this is simply helping people! Your insights can indirectly help people across the world or down the street! A simple video of you giving advice could go a long way! On top of that, the process of communicating what you know in new ways is a great learning opportunity for you. As you assess your knowledge base, you receive a fresh invitation to stay in-the-know and research the latest trends in the chiropractic industry.

Show your results

The stories and real-life results of chiropractic care are the absolute most effective selling point for your services! Customers actively seek out reviews and testimonials from their peers to make informed decisions. Solicit online reviews from your regular patients, and with their permission, share their testimonials on your website and social media pages! Don’t force your patients into giving positive reviews and don’t use fake testimonials!

The idea is to build trust, and as soon as your patients realize that that trust is built on a lie you’ll lose them! For a truly compelling story, consider filming a few video testimonials, as well as before-and-after video evidence that visually demonstrates the physical impact of chiropractic care, such as increased range of motion. However, you choose to collect them, share as many success stories as you can, and let your patients sell your practice on your behalf!

Develop partnerships in public relations

Think about all of the news broadcasting companies in your community — local TV stations, radio programs, newspapers, and magazines. Based on your public relations strategy, identify news outlets that you might be able to develop partnerships with.

Maybe you can drop in for a segment on the local news channel and discuss ergonomics in the workplace. The right magazine might be interested in a story about the physical side effects of using smartphones.

These partnerships can quickly turn into recurring gigs for you, and provide a significant level of exposure for your practice.

Invest in PPC advertising

Almost all of your customers are using the internet regularly, so digital advertising is an easy way to get noticed. Google offers pay-per-click advertising through AdWords, and just as the name suggests, you only pay when consumers choose to click on your advertisement. That said, there is still a lot of strategies involved in the PPC process, and it’s easy to waste your time and money if you’re not sure how to do it well. You could choose to do it on your own, but it’s not recommended unless you’re ready to learn an entirely new range of skills and are ready to sacrifice a lot of time, energy and money to make it happen. Increase visibility with traditional ads

We live in a digital world, but traditional advertising is anything but irrelevant! Think about the places your target demographic might be on a regular basis and consider placing a few ads accordingly. Keep in mind that this doesn’t always cost money! Grocery stores, coffee shops, and other local businesses are often open to display your business card or flyers to support the local business community. Traditional paid ads in newspapers, billboards, magazines, movie theaters and other outlets provide additional opportunities for increased visibility and business development.
