Understand your Unique Value Proposition

When a potential client visits your carpet cleaning website’s homepage, they need to learn quickly what makes your business stand out from all the rest. Your Unique Value Proposition (UVP) tells customers why they need to call you now instead of waiting, how you can help solve their problem, what makes your business unique among the competition. Many times, customers don’t actually know what they want when they begin looking for a service provider such as a carpet cleaner.

They have vague notions about getting help, but they are often not aware of all the possible services available to them. When they land on your homepage, your website should help them make a better decision about what they truly want or need and why yours is the best company to help them with that. Highlight what makes your carpet cleaning service unique. Tell them how you will save them time and money. Show them how your expertise will ensure a cleaner, healthier home for their family. Show them services they may not realize were offered and how these can help them solve a problem. Without this kind of targeted messaging, you’ll likely have many people visit your website but never follow through with a phone call or appointment. Convert more leads into sales by understanding your UVP and making sure it’s a focus on your homepage.

Embrace Email

and Social Media Marketing

Email marketing is a long-standing and effective way to generate new leads as well as support the retention of current customers. There are many automated email services today that can help you with these endeavors, and email campaigns are an inexpensive way to increase your sales. You can create different email messages for various subgroups, including people who are expressing interest for the first time, those looking for a niche or unusual service, those who have booked your services in the past, and those who have visited your website multiple times but never booked an appointment. Email campaigns are also a good way to give different promotions or discounts to different types of customers, so you can customize your motivation strategies.

Emails have been shown to increase customer retention, so your carpet cleaning business can benefit from repeat customers. Sending newsletters with helpful carpet or floor-care tips is often just the reminder people need to have their floors regularly maintained. These also show past customers you care about their business.

As much as many business owners would like to ignore social media, it is a necessary marketing tool in today’s world. Many channels, especially Facebook, offer a variety of advertising tactics to draw in customers to your business. Because they collect so much data on their users, you are better able to target ads to people who are most likely to book the services of a carpet cleaner in your area, including those who have recently moved, interior designers, and parents with young children.

If you maintain an active presence on social media, offering helpful information about floor care, new products, and design trends, this will increase the visibility of your brand as well as support your credibility in your market. While these efforts can be time-consuming, they do help generate new and continuing leads. Hiring support for social media may be worth it if you do not have time to engage in these efforts as a business owner.

Cultivate Great Reviews

There are two main ways people find new service providers, such as carpet cleaning services. The first is through internet research and the second is by referral. Many people will ask friends or family for recommendations when looking for services such as carpet cleaning, and nearly everyone will read online reviews of your carpet cleaners before booking your services. It is therefore important to cultivate great online reviews for your carpet cleaning business.

First, be sure you have claimed your business on Yelp and Google, keeping all information for your carpet cleaning business up to date. Check your reviews on these and other channels often to be sure they are accurate and to reach out when problems are reported in reviews. In your email campaigns and through social media, encourage customers to review your services on various platforms, and you can even provide them with step-by-step instructions on how to do this. Because most customers today trust online reviews, it’s important to be aware of what people are saying about you online and to encourage your satisfied customers to review you.
